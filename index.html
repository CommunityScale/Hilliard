Hilliard
Housing Study

The City of Hilliard has initiated a Housing Assessment, launched in August 2025 and led by our consultant team, CommunityScale. This effort will help us better understand the state of housing in Hilliard and identify opportunities to ensure our community continues to meet the needs of residents today and in the future. This page will be updated throughout the study process, with project background, updates, and ways to get involved.


What is the Housing Study?

The City of Hilliard is conducting a Housing Study to understand the kinds of housing Hilliard needs and wants - and how to get them built. The study is one step towards implementing the goals and vision for the City of Hilliard as outlined in Hilliard’s 2023 Community Plan. The Community Plan set forth a goal of making Hilliard a place where anyone can live; this Housing Study will clarify which types and affordability levels of housing are needed - and identify opportunities for where and how to achieve that vision.

Study goals:

Understand current housing conditions and future needs.
Engage residents and stakeholders to understand community priorities.
Identify housing production targets that meet local needs.
Develop recommendations for policy and planning improvements.

Initial Findings

We'll add draft findings here along the way

Recent growth trends.

Household growth has slowed considerably since 2019. The Community Plan reported that Hilliard’s growth since 2016 has been driven less by annexation than internal growth.

Household composition and structure.

These household types and living arrangements do not account for all households in Hilliard, but they do represent a variety of the types of households and people that call Hilliard home - who often prefer or need diverse housing types to accomodate their life stage.

The bottom chart shows which types of living arrangements have grown or shrunk across the Central Ohio region, to compare with those changes in Hilliard.

Population growth by age.

Since 2010, young adults and adults 50-64 have accounted for a large share of Hilliard's population growth. Almost 70% more seniors (adults 65+) call Hilliard home today than did in 2010.

Existing housing types.

The most common housing type in Hilliard is a single-family home with 4 or more bedrooms.

Housing affordability trends.

While typical home prices have historically been affordable to a median-income household, the typical home price is becoming unaffordable to more than half of this community's households. Many factors are at play, including overall increases in housing prices and interest rates.
